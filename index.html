👩‍🏫 For Parents & Teachers

Pizza Palace is a reading-comprehension game disguised as a cooking game. Every order is a short piece of English the child has to read, understand, and act on. The difficulty is in the language, not the dragging: orders grow from "a whole pizza with ham" to multi-sentence instructions with recipes, negations, conditionals, and spatial rules like "the two slices directly across from each other."

What it teaches

  • Reading carefully and following multi-step instructions in order.
  • Spatial and quantity language: half, quarter, every other, next to, opposite, except.
  • Ignoring distractions: friendly chatter and red-herring lines that must be read past, not followed.
  • Memory and inference: recalling a named recipe, or working out that "me, my wife and two kids" means four quarters.

Ages & levels

Twenty levels span roughly ages 4 to 12. The level adapts automatically: fast, accurate pizzas raise it; mistakes or running out of time lower it, so the game settles where the child is challenged but not stuck. There is nothing to configure.

The money is the gentle stakes

The shop starts with $20; each pizza costs $3 to make and accuracy pays it back, with a $1 tip for being quick and correct. A wrong pizza is refused. It teaches that reading carefully has a payoff, with no harsh "game over" sting beyond reopening the shop.

✅ Safe & private. No accounts, no sign-in, no ads, no chat, no in-app purchases. The game makes no internet connection at all while playing: progress is saved only in this browser, on this device, and nothing is ever sent anywhere. You can switch off the slang ("meme mode") and reset progress for a new player from inside the game.
